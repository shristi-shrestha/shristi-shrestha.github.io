People are sometimes surprised that "bioinformatics analyst" is a full-time service role, not just something a wet-lab scientist does on the side. At Creative Data Solutions (CDS), part of the Vanderbilt Center for Stem Cell Biology, that's exactly the model: our team provides bioinformatics and informatics research services to the broader Vanderbilt community.

## A typical request

A collaborator arrives with a question, not a pipeline - "does this transcription factor knockout change beta cell maturity?" or "what's driving the expression differences we're seeing between these two conditions?" Translating that into an actual analysis plan means picking the right assay (bulk vs. single-cell RNA-seq, sometimes ATAC-seq), the right statistical approach, and then - just as importantly - communicating the result back in a way that answers the biological question rather than just producing a plot.

## Range over depth

What I like about a shared analysis service is the range: one week it's islet biology, the next it could be a completely different tissue or disease model. It keeps the underlying skill set - quality control, differential expression, clustering, visualization - sharp, while constantly testing it against new biology. It's also pushed me to care more about reproducibility and clear documentation, since the person reading the analysis six months from now is rarely the person who wrote it.
